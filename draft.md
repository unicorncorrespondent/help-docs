Why one star
    The central theme of grief is presented shallowly.
    - five stage of grief:  denial, anger, bargaining, depression, and acceptance. The characters are in stage 2 and 4 statically, with no deeper dynamic going on. 
    - A good (and IMO, best) example of grief: Joan Didion's blue night, where she grief the death of her daughter. In her previous book, the year of magical thinking, which is also about mourning the death of her husband, she showed us how she went through the five stage of grief. In blue night, she went even deeper, into the realm of regret and guilt, and ultimately, how one completely reconstruct their story of life based on one thing, grief. This is the real power of grief. It's been presented in such elegent and vulunerable way.
    - the self-reflection, regret and guilt is so powerful that it moves us deeply. This is why the sun rises on us all (2025) inspired so much discussion within and beyond our panel.
    - Hamnet, compared to these works, has done nothing new with grief. I have no doubt that the actress Jessie Buckly might have experienced the power of grief and tried her best presenting, but the work itself greatly impede the performance and reduce the impact we should recieve from her acting.
    
    Building on the previous statement, I suggest that the strong emotion impact does not come from the movie itself.
